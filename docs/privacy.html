Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026

klipa is a clipboard manager and keep-awake utility that runs on your device. This privacy policy explains what information the app handles and what it does not do. It is short because there is very little to say.

Short version. klipa collects no personal data. Your clipboard history stays on your device, in your own user data folder. Nothing is uploaded, logged, synced, or sold. klipa has no servers, no analytics, no ads, no accounts, and no tracking.

Data stored on your device

klipa keeps a local history of items you have copied to the clipboard - text and images - so you can paste them back later. This history is written to a single JSON file, plus per-image PNG files for image entries, in your operating system's application data directory:

  • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/dev.peterdsp.klipa/
  • Windows: %APPDATA%\dev.peterdsp.klipa\
  • Linux: ~/.local/share/dev.peterdsp.klipa/

You can inspect, back up, or delete this folder at any time. The in-app Clear history action empties it for you.

What klipa never does

  • klipa never uploads your clipboard, or any part of it, to any server operated by us or anyone else.
  • klipa never contains any analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or tracking SDKs.
  • klipa never shows ads.
  • klipa never asks you to create an account.
  • klipa never syncs between devices.

Optional network requests

klipa is local-only by default. If you explicitly opt in to one of the optional menu bar modes that show a temperature, the app makes two kinds of anonymous, aggregate HTTP requests: one to ip-api.com to look up an approximate latitude and longitude from your IP address (cached for 24 hours), and one to open-meteo.com to fetch the current temperature at those coordinates (cached for 10 minutes). These requests are made only while a temperature mode is enabled and are not sent to us. The default menu bar mode makes no network requests at all.

Outside of the Mac App Store, klipa also checks the GitHub Releases API at most once a day to see if a newer version of klipa is available. This lets it show an "Update available" item in the menu. No information about you is sent in that request.

License activation (non-App-Store builds only)

The direct-download builds of klipa (macOS .pkg, Windows installer, Linux packages) include a paid unlock after a 7-day free trial. If you choose to activate a license key, klipa sends the key you paste to gumroad.com once, over HTTPS, to verify that it is valid. The result is cached locally so no further requests are needed to keep the app unlocked. The Mac App Store build does not include this feature at all.

Payment

On the Mac App Store, payment is handled entirely by Apple; klipa never sees your payment details. For the direct-download builds, payment is handled entirely by Gumroad, our merchant of record; klipa never sees your payment details either.

Children

klipa is a general-purpose utility and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, of any age.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes in any material way, the update will be shown here with a new "Last updated" date. The source of this page lives in the klipa GitHub repository, so every change is visible in the commit history.
